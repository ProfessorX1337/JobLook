Questions, answered honestly

What people ask before installing.

Does JobLook submit my applications for me?

No. JobLook fills fields and drafts answers — it never clicks submit. Every application is reviewed and submitted by you. This is a deliberate design choice: silent autosubmission is how people end up applying to the wrong company with the wrong cover letter, and we'd rather be slower and correct.

What happens to my resume and personal data?

Your resume is parsed into a structured profile and stored encrypted at rest with a per-user key. It's used only to fill fields and draft answers to long-form questions. We don't sell it, share it, or train third-party models on it. You can delete your profile from the dashboard at any time, and we'll wipe it within 24 hours.

Which job sites does it work on?

At launch: Greenhouse, Lever, and Ashby. Between them, those three power the majority of postings from mid-size companies, YC startups, and well-funded scale-ups — the employers most people actually want to work for. Workday, Taleo, and LinkedIn Easy Apply are on the roadmap but not available yet.

How does the AI draft a tailored answer?

When you're on a job posting, the extension reads the job description and pulls the relevant details from your profile — the experience, skills, and projects most relevant to what the role is asking for. It drafts an answer in your voice, matched to the question. You review every word before submitting. If the draft isn't right, you edit it or rewrite it. No draft ever leaves your browser unless you click submit.

Can I cancel Premium anytime?

Yes. Month-to-month or annual, cancel from your dashboard with one click. The 14-day free trial doesn't require a card until you decide Premium is worth it. Annual plans can be prorated on request if you cancel mid-term.

Will it get me a job?

No tool gets you a job. What JobLook does is remove the reason people stop applying — the soul-crushing repetition that makes application number twenty feel impossible when you wrote a great answer on application number two. If you're a strong candidate, applying to more of the right roles gets you more shots. That's the math.
