Autofill tools make one kind of mistake over and over: they put the right value in the wrong field. Your phone number ends up in the "how did you hear about us?" box. Your answer to "why this role?" gets pasted into the "additional information" slot.

That is a solvable problem. It takes thirty seconds and a habit. Before you submit any application — ours, anyone else's, or one you filled by hand — scan these six things.

## 1. Your name

Not a typo. Not "Alex Rivera" where one system truncated to "Alex". Not your legal name in the display slot if you go by something else. Not the capitalization a form auto-upper-cased on you. Look at the top of the application. Does it say who you actually are?

## 2. The email the company will actually reach

Recruiters email once. If it bounces, or lands in a mailbox you don't check, the role is gone. Check the email field. Is it the address you actually monitor? If you have a separate job-search inbox, is it pointed there?

## 3. The resume attached

Every autofill tool we've used — including older versions of ours — has occasionally attached the wrong resume variant. If you keep a "frontend" and a "backend" version, or a "manager" and an "IC" version, open the attached file. Confirm it's the one that matches the role.

## 4. The long-form answers, read aloud

Read each custom answer out loud. Not in your head — actually whisper it. AI drafts (ours included) tend to produce sentences that look fine on the page and sound strange in the mouth. If a sentence doesn't read naturally, delete it. One clean paragraph beats three polished-but-robotic ones.

## 5. The company name in the answer

This is the most common mistake. You reused an answer from a similar role, and you forgot to change "I'd love to join Stripe" to "I'd love to join Ramp." Search the text for the last company you applied to. If it appears, fix it.

## 6. The links

LinkedIn. Portfolio. GitHub. Personal site. Click each one. In a new tab, from the incognito version of the form if possible. Broken links and private-repo 404s are one of the few things that get flagged by both the ATS and the human reader.

## About salary and location

Two fields we won't autofill for you, because they're decisions, not data: expected salary and willing location. Take ten seconds on each. The range you answered last week for a startup Series A role is not the range you should answer this week for a public-company staff role.

## The one minute it costs

Thirty seconds to scan, another thirty if something needs fixing. Over a hundred applications, that's under two hours, and it will catch a handful of the "we loved you but your email bounced" stories that end every second job-search conversation we've had.

Build the habit now. Your future self — the one getting the callbacks — will thank you.
